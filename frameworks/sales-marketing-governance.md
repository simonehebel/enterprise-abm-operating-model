# Sales and Marketing Governance Framework

## Purpose

This framework defines how Sales and Marketing jointly manage account-based marketing priorities, decisions, execution and performance.

The goal is to create a practical operating model that connects:

- account selection,
- account ownership,
- customer insight,
- coordinated engagement,
- Sales follow-up,
- pipeline progression,
- investment decisions,
- continuous optimization.

Governance should reduce ambiguity and improve execution.

It should not create unnecessary meetings or approval layers.

## When to Use This Framework

Use this framework when an organization needs to:

- establish shared ownership of ABM,
- improve coordination between Sales and Marketing,
- clarify account and program responsibilities,
- create consistent review cadences,
- improve Sales follow-up,
- resolve conflicting account priorities,
- connect account engagement with pipeline outcomes,
- scale ABM across regions, teams or business units.

## 1. Governance Principles

A sustainable ABM governance model should follow these principles.

### Shared Business Outcomes

Sales and Marketing should align around account and revenue outcomes rather than isolated departmental metrics.

### Clear Decision Rights

Every major ABM decision should have a defined owner.

### Evidence-Based Prioritization

Account investment decisions should be supported by account fit, customer need, buying readiness and commercial potential.

### Consistent Execution

Teams should use common definitions, processes and review standards.

### Appropriate Cadence

The review frequency should reflect the account tier, Sales cycle and business urgency.

### Documented Actions

Every review should end with clear decisions, owners and deadlines.

### Sales Responsibilities

- relationship development,
- opportunity strategy,

- commercial qualification,
- follow-up,

- performance evaluation.

- resource decisions,

## 4. RACI Model

Use the following role definitions:

- **Responsible:** completes the work,
- **Accountable:** owns the final result or decision,
- **Consulted:** provides input,
- **Informed:** receives updates.

### Example RACI Matrix

| Activity | Marketing | Sales | Sales Operations | Leadership |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Define ABM objective | Responsible | Consulted | Consulted | Accountable |
| Develop account criteria | Responsible | Responsible | Consulted | Accountable |
| Select target accounts | Consulted | Responsible | Consulted | Accountable |
| Confirm account tiers | Responsible | Responsible | Consulted | Accountable |
| Conduct account research | Responsible | Consulted | Informed | Informed |
| Map buying committee | Consulted | Responsible | Informed | Informed |
| Develop messaging | Responsible | Consulted | Informed | Informed |
| Design engagement plan | Responsible | Consulted | Informed | Informed |
| Execute marketing programs | Responsible | Consulted | Informed | Informed |
| Follow up on engagement | Consulted | Responsible | Informed | Informed |
| Update opportunity status | Informed | Responsible | Accountable | Informed |
| Analyze performance | Responsible | Consulted | Responsible | Informed |
| Approve budget changes | Consulted | Consulted | Informed | Accountable |
| Decide tier movement | Responsible | Responsible | Consulted | Accountable |

The exact RACI model should reflect the organization's structure and Sales process.

## 5. Account Ownership

Every target account should have a clear Sales owner.

For priority accounts, also define:

- Marketing owner,
- regional owner,
- executive sponsor,
- partner owner,
- customer success owner,
- decision escalation path.

### Required Account Ownership Information

For each account, document:

- account name,
- account tier,
- Sales owner,
- Marketing owner,
- executive sponsor,
- current opportunity status,
- next-best action,
- review date.

## 6. Review Cadence

The governance cadence should reflect the level of account investment.

### Weekly Account Review

Recommended for:

- Tier 1 accounts,
- active opportunities,
- major buying signals,
- executive engagement,
- time-sensitive programs.

Topics:

- new engagement signals,
- buying committee activity,
- Sales follow-up,
- opportunity changes,
- immediate blockers,
- next-best actions.

### Monthly Program Review

Recommended for:

- Tier 1 and Tier 2 programs,
- regional account clusters,
- active campaigns,
- cross-functional execution.

Topics:

- account engagement,
- buying committee coverage,
- program performance,
- Sales response,
- opportunity creation,
- content effectiveness,
- resource requirements.

### Quarterly Business Review

Recommended for:

- strategic account portfolios,
- budget and resource allocation,
- account tiering,
- pipeline contribution,
- performance against business objectives.

Topics:

- strategic priorities,
- accounts to add or remove,
- tier movement,
- investment changes,
- pipeline and revenue impact,
- operational gaps,
- leadership decisions.

## 8. Sales Follow-Up Governance

Marketing engagement only creates value when relevant signals are acted upon.

The follow-up model should define:

- which signals require action,
- who receives the signal,
- expected response time,
- required follow-up action,
- feedback status,
- escalation process,
- recycling criteria.

### Example Follow-Up Standards

| Engagement Signal | Recommended Action | Owner | Expected Response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Executive event attendance | Personalized outreach | Sales | Within 2 business days |
| High-value content engagement | Validate customer need | Sales | Within 3 business days |
| Multiple buying roles engaged | Review account strategy | Sales and Marketing | Within 5 business days |
| Demo or meeting request | Direct qualification | Sales | Within 1 business day |
| No response after outreach | Return to nurture or adjust approach | Sales and Marketing | At next review |

These standards should be adapted to the organization's Sales model.

## 9. Shared Definitions

Governance requires common definitions.

### Target Account

An account that meets agreed strategic and commercial criteria.

### Engaged Account

An account showing relevant activity from one or more buying roles.

### Sales-Ready Account

An account with sufficient fit, need, engagement and timing to justify direct Sales action.

### Qualified Opportunity

An opportunity that meets the organization's agreed qualification criteria.

### Account Progression

Evidence that an account is moving toward a commercial decision or stronger relationship.

### Nurture Account

A strategically relevant account that is not currently ready for active Sales investment.

## 10. Escalation Model

Escalation is required when:

- account ownership is unclear,
- follow-up is repeatedly missed,
- Sales and Marketing disagree on priority,
- required resources are unavailable,
- executive support is needed,
- account risk increases,
- budget decisions are blocked.

### Escalation Process

1. Document the issue.
2. Define the business impact.
3. Identify the required decision.
4. Assign the decision owner.
5. Set a decision deadline.
6. Record the final outcome.

Escalation should focus on decisions rather than blame.

## 14. Governance Checklist

Before activating an ABM program, confirm:

- [ ] Business objective is agreed
- [ ] Target accounts are validated
- [ ] Account tiers are documented
- [ ] Sales ownership is confirmed
- [ ] Marketing ownership is confirmed
- [ ] Executive sponsor is identified where required
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities are documented
- [ ] Follow-up standards are agreed
- [ ] Shared definitions are documented
- [ ] Review cadence is scheduled
- [ ] Shared performance indicators are defined
- [ ] Escalation process is clear
- [ ] Decision log is available

## Key Principle

ABM governance should make execution easier, not slower.

An effective governance model connects:

- strategic account priorities,
- clear ownership,
- coordinated execution,
- timely follow-up,
- transparent decisions,
- measurable commercial outcomes.
